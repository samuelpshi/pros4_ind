# P3.8 delta-hedge test — does the noTrade-5100 alpha survive hedging?

Owner: P3.8_delta_hedge_test
Dates: 2026-04-26

## Setup

P3.7 forensics found that noTrade-5100's +390/day mean was largely
delta-1 PnL: D+2's +1256 came from EOD MTM on a +76-delta open position
(K=5000:+47, K=5300:+69, K=5500:−30) while S rallied +28 from open.
Closed round trips on D+2 totaled −75. The IV-scalp signal as a clean
PnL source was unproven. This phase runs one decisive test: enable a
minimal VEV delta hedge and re-backtest.

### Trader change — Module D (delta hedge), feature-flagged

`R3/traders/trader-r3-v1-vev-v1.5-moduleA-only.py` gained:

- New default config keys: `enable_delta_hedge` (bool, default
  **False**), `delta_hedge_band=30`, `delta_hedge_agg_band=60`,
  `delta_hedge_throttle_ticks=100`, `delta_hedge_max_step=30`,
  `vev_position_limit=200`.
- **Module D** block inserted between voucher-order emission and the
  paper-PnL/lambda-log section. When the flag is on:
  - Compute portfolio delta from CURRENT voucher positions
    (`state.position`, not targets) using per-strike BS delta evaluated
    at the per-tick smile fit. Add VEV current position (delta 1).
  - Throttle: at most one hedge action per `delta_hedge_throttle_ticks`.
  - Step cap: at most `delta_hedge_max_step` VEV per action.
  - Inside the `delta_hedge_band` (|delta| ≤ 30): no-op.
  - Outside the band but inside `delta_hedge_agg_band` (30 < |delta| ≤ 60):
    passive — post limit at our touch (uses `emit_passive_or_marketable`
    with `escalate=False`).
  - Outside `delta_hedge_agg_band` (|delta| > 60): aggressive —
    cross spread.
  - Clip to `vev_position_limit` (=200, per CLAUDE.md).
- Lambda log gained 5 new fields: `mD` (flag), `pf_d` (portfolio delta),
  `hdg_q` (signed hedge qty this tick), `hdg_a` (action fired flag),
  `hdg_p` (passive flag).

**Override of "no VEV trading" rule documented**: previous trader files
explicitly stated `# NOTE: no orders for VELVETFRUIT_EXTRACT (Module C
stripped).` Module D supersedes that rule when its flag is on. The
flag stays off in every existing config except the new
`vev_v1.5-moduleA-only-noTrade5100-hedged.json`. Module C (VEV
mean-reversion proper) is not being reintroduced — D only emits VEV
orders to neutralize voucher delta, never directionally.

## Verdict

The two verdict criteria from the task spec are **both nominally
satisfied**, which forces an interpretive call rather than a clean
classification:

- V4-confirmed criterion: "hedged closed-RT roughly matches unhedged
  closed-RT (both small/negative)" → **YES**. Closed-RT PnL is −86/3d
  in both runs, byte-identical (same voucher fills, same trip
  reconstruction).
- V4-rejected criterion: "hedged total PnL meaningfully positive
  (>+100/day)" → **NOMINALLY YES** at +155/day, but this is dominated
  by unhedged residual delta drift, not signal-side alpha.

### Verdict: **V4-confirmed**

Reasoning:

1. **Closed round trips don't make money.** Across 3 days the strategy
   completes 7 round trips (3 + 3 + 1) and they sum to −86 = −28.7/day.
   This is the cleanest measurement of "did the IV-scalp signal pay
   for itself?" — and the answer is no. D+2's only closed RT is a
   −75 loss at K=5000 (sold 3 at 268, bought back 3 at 293).
2. **The hedge fired exactly once on D+2 and captured 56% of the
   directional move.** 31 passive attempts on D+2 didn't fill (passive
   limits at our touch don't match this dataset's bot flow). Only the
   single aggressive cross-spread action when |pf_delta| crossed 60
   produced a fill, very late in the day (ts=951200).
3. **The +155/day total is mostly unhedged drift, not signal alpha.**
   Voucher MTM was +1171 = same as unhedged; VEV PnL was −705. The
   delta the hedge actually offset (~30 contracts × ~23.5 S move) is
   a small fraction of the gross directional drift the +47/+69/−30
   open position took on. Tightening the hedge (lower aggressive band,
   shorter throttle, higher max step) would push the closed-RT-vs-total
   gap closer to zero. We did not run that tighter test, but the
   direction is unambiguous from the lambda log: pf_delta was already
   in the 30–60 range for most of D+2 with no hedging happening.
4. **The voucher fill pattern is one-sided accumulation, not
   mean-reverting scalping.** D+2 has 73 voucher fills and 1 closed
   trip. Positions grow monotonically (K=5000 to +47, K=5300 to +69)
   without ever closing back through zero. Whatever signal `z<-2`
   provides at entry, the strategy never harvests it via reversion —
   z stays negative, position keeps growing, and EOD MTM does the
   accounting.

### IV-scalp edge is unproven. Reassess scalper viability.

Per spec: "V4-confirmed → IV-scalp edge is unproven; recommend
reassessing scalper viability."

The signal *exists in IV space* (Phase 3.6 audit: K=5000 residual stdev
0.00212 with K=5100 anchoring the smile) but the current trading
mechanics are not converting it to closed-trip PnL. Two failure modes
showing up:

- **Trip never closes (D+2 case)**: positions accumulate in the same
  direction; z stays beyond −z_close; min_hold + cooldown protect the
  open position from being flipped; EOD liquidation provides whatever
  PnL emerges from MTM.
- **Trip closes at a loss (D+0/D+1)**: the few completed RTs lose
  small amounts (−4, −7) because the residual moves against us between
  entry and the z-close threshold.

## Recommendations for next phase

1. **Do not proceed to a signal sweep on the current trader.** A sweep
   over `z_open × ema_demean × stdev_window` on this geometry will
   optimize for "configs that accumulate positions on a day where S
   trended favorably," not for IV-scalp signal quality. Hard Rule #4
   (don't optimize against a noisy metric) applies.
2. **Two reasonable next bets, ordered by tractability**:
   - **3.9a — Closing-logic redesign.** Keep the entry signal; replace
     hold/close logic with one or more of: (a) hard time-based exit
     after N ticks, (b) take-profit on residual halving, (c) wider
     z_close so the position closes earlier, (d) explicit max-hold
     enforced regardless of z. Goal: force the strategy to harvest
     reversion on signal-timescale rather than carry through full S
     drift. Test on closed-RT PnL as the primary metric, not total.
   - **3.9b — Tight delta hedge baseline.** Independent from 3.9a:
     re-run noTrade-5100 with `delta_hedge_band=10`,
     `delta_hedge_agg_band=15`, `delta_hedge_throttle_ticks=20`,
     `delta_hedge_max_step=10`. If even a tight hedge leaves total
     PnL positive (closed-RT + small residual), there is real
     non-directional alpha worth preserving. If total PnL collapses
     to closed-RT (−29/day), there isn't.
3. **Pivot option (only if 3.9a + 3.9b both null)**: revert to
   baseline (6 active strikes, 6 fit, z_open=2.0) — but acknowledge
   that's a worse-mean strategy that just hides the same problem under
   different strike weights. The structural issue (Hard Rule #8 not
   followed; positions accumulate without close) is not strike-set
   dependent.
4. **Phase-3 sweep scoring note (replaces the open scoring question
   from P3.7)**: with V4 confirmed, do not run a broad signal sweep
   until either 3.9a or 3.9b shows a path to positive closed-RT PnL.
   Once that exists, score by closed-RT mean − closed-RT stdev. Total
   PnL as a metric is too contaminated by directional drift on this
   architecture.
