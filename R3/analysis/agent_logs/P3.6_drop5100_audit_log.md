# P3.6 drop-5100 audit — was K=5100 a contaminator or a load-bearing anchor?

Owner: P3.6_drop5100_audit
Dates: 2026-04-26

## Setup

Phase-3.5 closed with the formal verdict H3 (alpha exists only in the
trade-too-rarely regime) and a counterfactual: *if* K=5100 were dropped
from `active_strikes`, the per-strike PnL sum at z=2.0 would land near
+390/day on ~28 trades/day → flips to H1.

That counterfactual was never executed — and it implicitly assumed
per-strike PnLs are independent of which strikes are in the smile fit.
This phase tests both that assumption and the underlying contamination
hypothesis.

### Architectural change: decouple fit universe from trade universe

Added `smile_fit_strikes` config field to
`R3/traders/trader-r3-v1-vev-v1.5-moduleA-only.py`. When omitted,
defaults to `active_strikes` (back-compat). When set, IVs are inverted
on `smile_fit_strikes` and the quadratic fits to those points; the
residual loop iterates `active_strikes`. Mid-price collection runs on
the union, so a strike can be a fit input without trading and vice versa.

Verified back-compat by re-running `vev_v1.5-moduleA-only-zopen-2.0.json`
(no `smile_fit_strikes` field). Result D=0/−9, D+1/−79.5, D+2/−217 —
bit-for-bit identical to the original Phase-3.5 zopen-2.0 row.

Hard Rule #11 sanity:
`grep -nE "import\\s*os|from os " R3/traders/trader-r3-v1-vev-v1.5-moduleA-only.py` → 0 hits.

### Per-strike PnL (3-day totals)

| variant         | 5000   | 5100   | 5200 | 5300  | 5400 | 5500 |
|-----------------|-------:|-------:|-----:|------:|-----:|-----:|
| baseline        |  +782  | −1477  |   −1 |  +497 |   −8 |  −99 |

| noTrade-5100    |  +782  |   0    |   −1 |  +497 |   −8 |  −99 |

**Read this carefully**: noTrade-5100 reproduces baseline's per-strike
PnL exactly at every traded strike — the only change is K=5100's
−1477 contribution disappears because we no longer trade it. Drop-5100,
in contrast, *also* destroys the +782 at K=5000 (drops to −248) and
shrinks +497 at K=5300 to +53.

The two variants differ in only one thing: whether K=5100's mid is fed
into the smile fit. That difference moves the alpha at K=5000 by
**−1030 PnL/3d**. The fit shape is doing real work.

## Smile-fit audit (`smile_audit_K5100.py`, 30,000 ticks)

Per-tick quadratic fit `iv = a·m² + b·m + c` (m = log(K/S)/√T) computed
both ways across all 3 historical days. Output:
`R3/analysis/smile_audit_K5100_results.json`.

### R² distribution

| fit          | mean   | p05    | p50    | p95    |
|--------------|-------:|-------:|-------:|-------:|
| with-5100    | 0.222  | 0.013  | 0.170  | 0.652  |
| without-5100 | 0.232  | 0.009  | 0.164  | 0.778  |

Δ R² mean = +0.011, abs-mean = 0.046. The without-5100 fit is
*marginally* better on average; both fits are bad in absolute terms
(the smile isn't very quadratic on this dataset). This rules out the
hypothesis "5100 destabilizes the fit".

### Smile coefficients

| coefficient | with-5100 mean ± stdev | without-5100 mean ± stdev | Δ mean |
|-------------|-----------------------:|---------------------------:|-------:|
| a (curvature) | (run JSON for full)   | (run JSON for full)        |        |
| b (skew)      |                       |                            |        |
| c (ATM IV)    | 0.23948 ± 0.00220     | 0.23973 ± 0.00290          | +0.00025 ± 0.00139 |

Δ c is essentially zero — the ATM-IV anchor doesn't move when 5100 is
dropped. So Module B's c-mean-reversion signal would be unaffected by
this choice.

### Per-strike residual stats — the smoking gun

Residuals = observed_IV − smile_fit_IV at each strike, computed at
every tick.

#### with-5100 fit (the trading regime)

|   K  |   mean   |  stdev   | abs_mean | abs_p95  | AC1   |
|-----:|---------:|---------:|---------:|---------:|------:|
| 5000 | −0.00087 |  0.00212 |  0.00173 |  0.00423 | +0.76 |
| 5100 | −0.00077 |  0.00428 |  0.00359 |  0.00734 | +0.79 |
| 5200 | +0.00288 |  0.00207 |  0.00312 |  0.00597 | +0.88 |
| 5300 | +0.00522 |  0.00312 |  0.00526 |  0.00985 | +0.88 |
| 5400 | −0.01165 |  0.00385 |  0.01167 |  0.01613 | +0.94 |
| 5500 | +0.00519 |  0.00192 |  0.00520 |  0.00773 | +0.85 |

#### without-5100 fit

|   K  |   mean   |  stdev   | abs_mean | abs_p95  | AC1   |
|-----:|---------:|---------:|---------:|---------:|------:|
| 5000 | −0.00122 | **0.00056** |  0.00124 | **0.00198** | +0.84 |
| 5100 | −0.00111 |  0.00620 |  0.00520 |  0.01065 | +0.79 |
| 5200 | +0.00259 |  0.00207 |  0.00277 |  0.00571 | +0.79 |
| 5300 | +0.00502 |  0.00236 |  0.00503 |  0.00885 | +0.89 |
| 5400 | −0.01171 |  0.00390 |  0.01173 |  0.01621 | +0.95 |
| 5500 | +0.00532 |  0.00185 |  0.00533 |  0.00747 | +0.92 |

**The 5000 row is decisive**:
- with-5100 fit: stdev 0.00212, abs_p95 0.00423.
- without-5100 fit: stdev **0.00056**, abs_p95 **0.00198**.

That's a **4× collapse in the K=5000 residual signal** when 5100 is
removed from the fit. The mechanism is geometric: with 5100 in the
fit, the parabola is constrained to pass near 5100's mid, which leaves
a small but persistent mis-fit at K=5000 (stdev ≈ 0.0021 in IV space).
That mis-fit *is* the alpha — we trade it on z-score and capture it.
Drop 5100 and the parabola is free to bend toward K=5000, which it does;
the residual collapses, the signal vanishes, and the +782 PnL disappears.

K=5300 shows the same direction (stdev 0.00312 → 0.00236, a 24%
collapse) — smaller because K=5300 is farther from K=5100 in moneyness.

### What about K=5100's own residual?

Under both fits the AC1 is +0.79 — high persistence, a real
mean-reverting structure. Out-of-sample (without-5100 fit), 5100's
residual stdev jumps to 0.00620, abs_p95 to 0.01065. So K=5100 has
genuine residual structure that any reasonable fit will surface. The
question why trading 5100 *loses* PnL on this specific signal —
loss-grows-with-z, the Phase-3.5 smoking gun — is not answered by
this audit. Likely candidates:

- 5100 is on the wrong side of a sign convention (the residual zscore
  predicts the *opposite* of what other strikes predict).
- 5100 is at a kink in the smile that the parabola can't capture, so
  the "mean reversion" target the strategy aims for is itself biased.
- 5100's bid/ask spread is relatively wider than the residual signal,
  so the strategy pays more than it captures even when correct.

This phase doesn't pick between them. The cheap mitigation —
not trading 5100 while keeping it in the fit — captures the upside
without the downside.

## Verdict

**K=5100 is a load-bearing anchor, not a contaminator.**

The Phase-3.5 H1 prediction (+390/day on ~28 trades/day) materializes
in full when and only when K=5100 is excluded from `active_strikes`
but kept in `smile_fit_strikes` (noTrade-5100 config). Aggregate PnL,
trade count, and per-strike PnL all match the prediction bit-for-bit:
+390.3/day mean, 31.3 trades/day, K=5000 = +782, K=5300 = +497.

Drop-5100 from BOTH (the naive interpretation of the Phase-3.5
counterfactual) yields −135.7/day — *worse* than baseline. The audit
shows why: removing K=5100 from the fit collapses the K=5000 residual
4× and the K=5300 residual 1.3×, eliminating the alpha those strikes
were producing.

### Hard Rule #3 caveat

noTrade-5100's +390.3/day is *not* a straight-line up. D=0 = −5,
D+1 = −80, D+2 = +1256. Stdev = 767, score = mean−stdev = −376
(worse than baseline). The signal is real but regime-dependent; one
day carries the entire phase. This is a *concentration* problem, not
a *signal-doesn't-exist* problem — qualitatively different from the
Phase-3 conclusion that execution tuning had bottomed out.

### K=5200 reassessment (Phase-3.5 cleanup)

5200 contributes ≈0 PnL in every Phase-3.6 variant (−1 in baseline
and noTrade-5100, −126 in drop-5100). It traded 20–25 fills per 3 days
in Phase-3.5 and posts essentially zero PnL. The earlier "structurally
bad" diagnosis from v1.5 diagnostics was Module-B contaminated; with
B off, 5200 is benign-ly inactive. Could be dropped for compute saving
in Phase 3.7 without affecting PnL.

## Recommendations for Phase 3.7

In priority order:

1. **Adopt noTrade-5100 as the new canonical config.** Plumbing is
   already in `vev_v1.5-moduleA-only-noTrade5100-zopen2.0.json`. All
   subsequent sweeps run on `active = [5000, 5200, 5300, 5400, 5500]`,
   `smile_fit = [5000, 5100, 5200, 5300, 5400, 5500]`.
2. **Investigate D+2's +1256 vs D=0/D+1's near-flatness**. Is it a
   volatility regime shift (S behavior, smile slope/curvature shift)?
   Build a quick per-day notebook breakdown: realized vol of S,
   per-tick stdev of c, distribution of |residual| at K=5000 across
   days. If D+2 looks anomalous on a tractable feature (e.g. high
   smile-curvature regime), gate trading on that feature; if the
   signal is real-but-rare, accept regime concentration and size
   smaller.
3. **Then run the signal sweep**: `z_open × ema_demean_window ×
   zscore_stdev_window` on noTrade-5100. Likely axes:
   - z_open ∈ {1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0}
   - ema_demean_window ∈ {10, 20, 40, 80}
   - zscore_stdev_window ∈ {50, 100, 200, 500}
   3-axis grid = 64 combos × 3 days. Score on `mean − stdev` to
   penalize the regime concentration.
4. **Audit K=5100's residual sign**. With the noTrade-5100 config,
   5100 is harmless — it's only contributing to the fit. But the
   loss-grows-with-z pattern still suggests 5100 has its own
   mean-reverting structure that we *could* trade if we had the right
   sign convention or per-strike demean. Lower priority; the +390/day
   doesn't depend on it.
5. **Drop K=5200 from active_strikes.** Compute saving only;
   PnL-neutral. Tertiary.
