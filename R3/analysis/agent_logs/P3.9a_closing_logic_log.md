# P3.9a closing-logic redesign — does a different exit fix closed-RT?

Owner: P3.9a_closing_logic
Dates: 2026-04-26
Status: **No-improvement.** Surfaced to Sam, no auto-pivot.

## Setup

P3.10a's forward-residual-PnL test confirmed the IV-residual signal
has positive edge at h=20–200 ticks (top |z| bucket: +0.81 per-tick
to +1.66 refit-20, in residual units). P3.8 hedged closed-RT was
−86/3d. Hypothesis under test in this phase: the gap between
"signal exists at h=200" and "closed-RT loses" is the exit rule.
`z_close=0.5` requires ~75% reversion; if typical reversion is
smaller, positions sit unclosed until EOD.

## Trader change

`R3/traders/trader-r3-v1-vev-v1.5-moduleA-only.py`:

- New config keys: `exit_mode` (str, default `"legacy"`),
  `hold_ticks` (int, default 200).
- Module A's exit branch refactored:
  - `legacy` / `loose_z`: `|z| < z_close` only. (`loose_z` is just a
    config-name convention — same code path, larger `z_close`.)
  - `time_based`: ignore z; close after `hold_ticks` ticks since
    open. The "open tick" is recorded per strike when prev=0 and
    a new entry is desired (or on outright sign flip).
  - `hybrid`: close when `|z| < z_close` OR `hold_ticks` elapsed,
    whichever first.
- `min_hold` / `cooldown` gating preserved across modes.
- `open_ticks` dict added to `saved`. Reset to current tick on
  fresh-from-flat opens and on sign flips. Dropped on full close.
- Lambda log gained `exm` (exit-mode tag) and `cls` (per-K close-
  trigger this tick: `"z"` or `"t"`).

Hard Rule #11 verified: zero `os` references. Syntax clean.

## Why didn't the P3.10a forward-PnL prediction hold?

P3.10a's forward-residual-PnL test measured `−sign(z[t]) × (resid
[t+h] − resid[t])` on **mid-price residuals**. It found +1.48 per
event in top |z| bucket at h=200 (ema-100 regime). That number is
in residual units, on mids, ignoring fill mechanics.

The closed-RT test sees something different. Three plausible reasons
the diagnostic edge doesn't survive:

1. **Adverse-selection at entry.** Our orders only fill on |z|>2
   when a counterparty is willing to lift our quote at the wide
   touch. Those counterparties have information; the residual at
   our actual fill price is worse than the mid-residual the
   diagnostic measured. The signal-on-mid edge gets eaten by the
   mid-vs-fill spread cost.
2. **Step-cap dispersion.** `max_step=5` + `passive_wait=8` means
   even a 60-share entry/exit lands across many ticks at varying
   residuals. The diagnostic conditions on a single instant; the
   trader realizes a smear of prices across the reversion path.
3. **Exit fires before peak reversion.** With z_close=0.5, exit
   triggers when residual returns to within 0.5 stdev of EMA. On
   half the events the residual continues past 0 and back the
   other way; z_close fires on the first crossing, possibly the
   wrong side of zero. Loosening to z_close=1.0 made this worse,
   not better — earlier closes at less-reverted residuals.

(1) is the most damning. Time-based exits (which sidestep z entirely)
catastrophically failed (−734) — because forced exits at uniform-in-
time residuals are no better than coin flips minus spread.

## What the data says about the signal

Of the 7 closed RTs in legacy, 4 lose with mean −25.8 and 3 win
with mean +5.7. Net: **mean closed RT = −12 / trade**. Per-event
this is negative. Across 7 round trips on 3 days, that's −86 total.
The signal does not produce profitable closed round trips at any
exit policy tested.

The +466 total PnL on legacy is composed of:
- closed RTs: −86
- EOD MTM on open positions: +552

The +552 is exactly the V4-confirmed delta drift (S rallied +28 on
D+2 → +76 net delta open at EOD ≈ +782 unhedged; hedge stripped
~+316 of that, leaving +466 surplus). That's not signal alpha —
that's the same residual directional bet P3.7/P3.8 found.

## What this surfaces

The signal as currently constructed does not produce profitable
closed round trips, regardless of exit rule. P3.10a's diagnostic
showed forward-PnL edge on mids, but the trader doesn't trade mids:

- entries fill at the touch (or worse, escalated cross-spread)
- exits fill at the touch (or worse, escalated)
- step-cap and passive-wait disperse fills

The most likely binding constraint is **adverse-selection at
entry**: only the counterparties who *want* to take our quote at
extreme |z| will fill, and on average they have an edge against us.

Three candidate next directions, in increasing intrusiveness:

1. **Entry-side filter — bid-ask spread gate.** Only enter when
   the touch spread is ≤ N ticks (e.g. N=1 or 2). Wide spreads at
   |z|>2 events are a tell that the book is one-sided. Cheap test.
2. **3.10b — smile smoothing in the trader.** P3.10a showed
   smoothing roughly doubles the mid-edge; even if some is eaten
   by adverse selection, may be enough to flip closed-RT.
3. **Architecture pivot.** If the residual signal cannot produce
   positive closed-RT with reasonable trader changes, this strategy
   is not viable. Consider HYDROGEL_PACK delta-1 (untouched in R3
   so far) or VEV mean-reversion as the primary R3 P&L source, and
   demote the IV-scalp work to a later round.
