## Hypothesis classification

Per the strict rubric:

- **H1** requires PnL to improve up to a plateau/peak AND ≥1 z_open
  to produce both positive PnL and >30 trades/day across all strikes.
  *Aggregate fails*: at z=2.0 (37 trades/day) PnL is −101.8/day, still
  negative.
- **H2** requires PnL to be flat or worsen monotonically. *Fails*:
  z=1.5 → 2.0 is a +271/day improvement, and z=3.5 crosses zero.
- **H3** requires PnL to be positive only when trade count collapses
  (<10 trades/day). *Holds*: only z=3.5 (0.7 trades/day) posts
  positive aggregate PnL.

**Formal answer: H3.** Real edge exists only in a regime too thin to
trade economically *as currently configured (all 6 strikes active)*.

### But the per-strike picture rewrites the story

The H3 verdict is a **strike-mix artifact**. The signal *does* have
real edge on at least 5000 and 5300 — both flip from negative to
strongly positive between z=1.5 and z=2.0, while 5100 explodes in the
opposite direction and dominates the aggregate. **If 5100 were dropped
from `active_strikes`, the result at z=2.0 would be +390/day on ~28
trades/day across the remaining 5 strikes — squarely H1.**

That counterfactual cannot be ratified here without actually running
the strike-drop variant; surfacing it as the obvious next test rather
than improvising a fix in this phase. (See workflow_preferences:
unexpected behavior → stop and ask.)

## Recommendations for next phase

In priority order:

1. **Run a "drop 5100" diagnostic.** One backtest at z=2.0, pw=8,
   az=3.5, ms=5, `active_strikes = [5000, 5200, 5300, 5400, 5500]`.
   If it lands at +390/day-ish with ~28 trades/day, the conclusion
   flips from H3 to H1 and a full signal sweep becomes warranted.
   This is the *cheapest* next experiment.
2. **Audit the 5100 residual signal** before any sweep that includes
   it. Inverting the sign of 5100's z (or using its own per-strike
   demean window) is the kind of change that should be motivated
   by a real diagnosis, not a hack — but the smoking gun (loss
   *grows* with stricter threshold) suggests a smile-fit
   contamination at 5100's strike specifically. Cross-check against
   the per-tick smile coefficients in the lambda log on a few
   high-loss windows.
3. **Do NOT run a full signal sweep yet.** A signal sweep over
   `z_open × ema_demean_window × zscore_stdev_window × smile_fit_method`
   while 5100 is still poisoning the aggregate would just be more
   confounded measurement. Drop 5100 (or fix it) first.
4. Re-think 5200's role only after the active-strike set is settled.
   It currently just sits there harmlessly; whether to drop it for
   compute or keep it as filler is a tertiary concern.
